On Rowbound, compliance metadata isn't a separate spreadsheet — it's attached to the data itself.

## What travels with a delivery

Every delivered dataset comes with a **manifest**:

- **Source** — `request`, `collect`, or `catalog`: how the records were produced.
- **License** — the license name and terms, resolved from the request or listing.
- **Provenance** — where the supplier says the data came from.
- **Consent basis** — for field-collected personal or location data, the consent/lawful basis captured per entry.
- **Dataset hash & record count** — integrity and exactly what you paid for.

## Why it matters

- **Buyers** get an audit trail for every record — what you can do with it, and where it came from.
- **Suppliers** who carry clean provenance and licensing command higher prices and clear review faster.
- **The platform** can act fast on problems: a flagged dataset can be taken down and its delivery links revoked within seconds.

License and provenance are part of how Rowbound stays trustworthy at scale — and a genuine differentiator versus browse-and-hope catalogs.
